1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Elite Floors ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website (the “Service”). This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree to our use of cookies, you should adjust your browser settings accordingly or discontinue use of our website.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites function efficiently and to provide information to website operators.

Cookies can be categorized as:

  • Session cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a specified period or until deleted
  • First-party cookies: Cookies set by the website you are visiting
  • Third-party cookies: Cookies set by domains other than the website you are visiting

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core features such as security, accessibility, and network management. These cookies cannot be disabled.

Examples include:

  • Cookie consent: Stores your cookie preferences
  • Session management: Maintains your session while browsing the website

3.2 Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting information anonymously. This allows us to analyze traffic and improve performance and usability.

Examples include:

  • Google Analytics: Tracks page views, traffic sources, and user behavior
  • Performance monitoring: Helps identify technical issues and optimization opportunities

3.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization. They may be set by us or by third-party service providers integrated into our website.

Examples include:

  • Language preferences: Remembers your selected language
  • Form data: Temporarily stores form inputs to improve user experience

3.4 Marketing Cookies

Marketing cookies may be used to track visitors across websites in order to display relevant advertisements and measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns.

Examples include:

  • Advertising networks: Track ad performance and engagement
  • Social media platforms: Enable content sharing and tracking

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that place cookies on your device, including but not limited to Google Analytics, Google Ads, and social media platforms. These third parties may collect information about your online activities across different websites.

We do not control third-party cookies. For more information, please review the privacy policies of the respective third-party providers.

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have the right to accept or reject cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to decline or delete cookies if you prefer.

5.1 Browser Settings

You can manage cookies through your browser settings. Instructions are available for:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

You may opt out of certain third-party cookies using tools such as:

  • Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on
  • Digital Advertising Alliance
  • Network Advertising Initiative

Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may affect your experience on our website, and some features may not function correctly.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer a “Do Not Track” (DNT) feature. Currently, there is no universally accepted standard for interpreting DNT signals. Our website does not currently respond to DNT signals.
